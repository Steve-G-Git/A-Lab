THE PERSON BEHIND THE TERMINAL

About
Steve

I come from a background in precision manufacturing, specifically the production of polycrystalline diamond compact inserts used in industrial drill bits at US Synthetic. It’s an environment where tolerances are measured in microns, mistakes are expensive, and consistency matters. That kind of pressure builds discipline, attention to detail, and a habit of solving problems methodically.

That mindset is what I bring into IT.

In manufacturing, I worked within Lean and the Toyota Production System, focusing on identifying inefficiencies, standardizing processes, and eliminating waste. In IT, that translates directly into how I approach troubleshooting: isolate variables, test assumptions, document results, and refine the process until the issue is resolved and repeatable.

I've always been drawn to computers and technology — the kind of person who enjoys figuring out how things work under the hood. Making the move into IT felt less like a leap and more like a deliberate move. I'm not just studying the field — I actively build and experiment with projects like a browser-based terminal lab, using AI-assisted tools and then breaking down how they work to understand the underlying behavior.

I’m currently studying for my CompTIA A+ certification while building hands-on labs and projects to reinforce what I learn. I don’t rely on theory alone. If I study a concept, I implement it, test it, and make sure I can explain and reproduce it.

Based in Tooele, Utah, I’m working toward an entry-level IT support role where I can apply structured problem-solving, strong fundamentals, and a bias toward action in a real environment.

LEAN / TPS BACKGROUND

Trained in Lean manufacturing principles with hands-on experience applying the Toyota Production System in a high-precision industrial environment. Focused on waste elimination, process standardization, and building cultures of continuous improvement.
